Epistemic Power

Who Gets to Define the Global?

A Citation Power Map of Global Studies Scholarship

A Gephi network visualisation mapping citation patterns across 20+ foundational global studies texts, revealing that a field claiming to study the globe concentrates overwhelmingly in European and North American institutional voices.

Discursive Power

The Language of Occupation

Computational Text Analysis of UN Resolutions on Kashmir, 1948–2019

A Voyant Tools corpus analysis of all UN Security Council resolutions on Jammu & Kashmir, tracking how the language of self-determination was gradually replaced by the language of stability and counterterrorism over seven decades.

Voyant Tools Text Analysis UN Corpus

Colonial Power

Europe's Others

A Spatial History of Colonial Knowledge Extraction

A StoryMapJS spatial argument tracing ten moments where European institutions — Kew Gardens, the British Museum, the Bodleian Library — extracted knowledge, texts, and cultural objects from colonised territories and incorporated them into European intellectual authority.

StoryMapJS Spatial History Colonial Archives

About This Research

I am a postcolonial and digital humanities researcher based in Srinagar, Kashmir. My primary research focuses on Kashmiri and Urdu women's literary culture — specifically on Dr. Tarannum Riyaz, the founding figure of Kashmiri women's Urdu fiction — and on the All-India Progressive Writers' Movement as a decolonial intellectual network.

I hold degrees in English Literature, Psychology, and Philosophy, and am currently completing an MA in Philosophy at IGNOU. I have worked as an Administrative Officer in the Government of Jammu and Kashmir, where I gained direct experience of how state epistemology produces and distorts social knowledge.

The projects collected here form part of a larger research programme on how global knowledge frameworks determine which literary and intellectual traditions are made legible — and on what terms.
