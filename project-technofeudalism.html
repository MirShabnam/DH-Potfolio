Feudalism to
Technofeudalism

Power, Hierarchy, and Digital Culture

A cultural studies analysis arguing that the logic of digital platform capitalism is a structural continuation of historical feudalism — that data rent, algorithmic control, and platform dependency reproduce the power structures of an older economic order in a new register.

Argument

Feudalism concentrated power through control of land — the essential resource of the agricultural economy. Technofeudalism concentrates power through control of data and platforms — the essential resources of the digital economy. In both systems, the majority do not own the means of production. They rent access to it, generating value that flows upward to owners.

The vassal pays rent in grain. The platform user pays rent in data and attention. The structural logic is the same.

Key Finding

"Calling the digital economy a 'disruption' of older hierarchies is itself an ideological move — one that makes the structural continuity invisible. The most powerful digital platforms have recreated the feudal logic of rent-extraction and dependency more effectively than any 20th century monopoly."
